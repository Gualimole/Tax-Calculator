Future Directions

Tax-Calculator development has both near-term and long-term objectives.

Near-Term Objective

The near-term objective is the development of other modules that can be used along with Tax-Calculator to conduct non-static tax analysis that generates both aggregate and distributional reform results. Three other modules are planned:

1. Partial-equilibrium Behavioral-Response capabilities have been completed.

2. Business-Taxation capabilities are being developed that will permit the effects of reforms in (corporate and pass-through) business taxes to feed back to filing-unit incomes, and therefore, individual taxes, in Tax-Calculator as envisioned in this note.

3. A Macro-Feedback capability is being planned that will allow the macroeconomic growth effects of a tax reform to feed back to filing-unit incomes, and therefore, individual taxes, in Tax-Calculator.

Once Tax-Calculator can support these other modules, it is anticipated that the public application programming interface (API) of Tax-Calculator will be stable.

Long-Term Objectives

Fix bugs in tax-calculation logic.

Add new policy parameters to enable parametric simulation of new reforms.

Add actual policy parameter values as they become available each year.

Update filing-unit data used by Tax-Calculator as they become available.

Improve model documentation.
